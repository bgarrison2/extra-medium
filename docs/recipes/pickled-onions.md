#Pickled red onions
Pickled red onions go on top of almost everything we eat and as a result, we burn through jars of these things. It's good that they are very easy to make. This recipe is very basic. I like the ratio of sugar and salt, but you can play with those, and you can also get fancy. Try a teaspoon of turmeric if you only have white onions - it makes cool yellow onions. Use less sugar to make your own movie nacho style pickled jalpenos. Add other seasonings for complexity.

##Ingredients
* 1-2 red onions

* 1 cup of water

* 1 cup of white vinegar

* .5 cup of sugar

* 1-2 tbsp salt

* bay leaf

* 1 quart wide-mouth mason jar with a screw top lid

##Steps
1. Cut up enough red onions to fill the mason jar and add your bay leaf. I like to halve the onion, slice it radially, halve the half onion again, and then cut off the root end. Nice bite-sized pieces of onions.

2. In a saucepan, combine water, vinegar, salt, and sugar. Ensure salt and sugar dissolve and bring to a boil.

3. Pour brine over red onions in mason jar, screw the lid on, and wait. They should turn nice and pink.
